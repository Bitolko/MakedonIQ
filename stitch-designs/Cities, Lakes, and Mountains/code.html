Cities, Lakes, and Mountains

Discover the majestic landscapes and vibrant urban hearts of Macedonia, from the "Jerusalem of the Balkans" to the peaks of Mavrovo.

menu_book Overview

Macedonia is a landlocked country defined by its dramatic topography. To the west, the Dinaric Alps rise sharply, while the central valleys host the Vardar River. The country's "Blue Eyes"—Lakes Ohrid, Prespa, and Dojran—serve as vital ecological and cultural hubs. Understanding these natural landmarks is the first step to truly knowing the Macedonian spirit.

Did you know?

Lake Ohrid is one of Europe's deepest and oldest lakes, estimated to be between 1 and 4 million years old. It hosts more than 200 endemic species that are found nowhere else in the world!
